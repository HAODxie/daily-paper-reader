## 摘要
背景：精神分裂症是一种严重的神经精神疾病。通过无创神经影像学方法描述其潜在生物学机制并建立诊断标记的工作仍在进行中，由此产生了一系列基于大脑的理论框架。关于静息态功能磁共振成像（rsfMRI）中与精神分裂症相关异常功能连接的主要框架包括连接失调假说、认知代谢障碍理论和三重网络理论。尽管这些先前的研究提供了信息，但可以通过增加样本量、避免确认偏倚、考虑个体差异以及药物和病程的影响来改进。

方法：基于这些建议，我们采用数据驱动的全脑方法，使用一个大型多站点rsfMRI数据集（N = 2,656；精神分裂症患者=1,248）。我们使用参考引导的独立成分分析生成个体水平的全脑功能网络连接（FNC），并提取与精神分裂症模式相似性的影像标记。我们模拟了药物剂量、发病年龄、病程、症状严重程度和认知表现与FNC之间的关系。

结果：我们的分析识别出一个可靠的精神分裂症-FNC特征，其特征是异常较强的小脑-丘脑负连接和丘脑-皮质正连接，涉及感觉、运动和联合皮层回路。虽然药物和病程与这些特征显著相关，但核心的小脑-丘脑破坏仍然是精神分裂症的稳健标记。

结论：这项工作是迄今为止规模最大的精神分裂症特异性rsfMRI研究，通过更细致的临床变量如何与大脑连接相互作用的图谱，完善了现有的理论框架，并提供了与精神分裂症相关连接的高保真模板。我们已将该模板作为开放获取资源发布，以促进可重复性并加速基于rsfMRI的可靠精神分裂症生物标记的开发。

## Abstract
BackgroundSchizophrenia is a severe neuropsychiatric disorder. Efforts to describe the underlying biology and establish diagnostic markers through non-invasive neuroimaging methods are ongoing, resulting in a range of theoretical brain-based frameworks. Prominent frameworks for aberrant schizophrenia-associated functional connectivity in resting-state functional magnetic resonance imaging (rsfMRI) include the dysconnectivity hypothesis, theory of cognitive dysmetria, and triple network theory. Although informative, prior work can be improved by increasing sample size, avoiding confirmation bias, and accounting for individual variability and the effects of medication and chronicity.

MethodsWith these recommendations in mind, we employed a data-driven, whole-brain approach using a large multi-site rsfMRI dataset (N = 2,656; schizophrenia = 1,248). We used reference-guided independent component analysis to generate subject-specific whole-brain functional network connectivity (FNC) and extract imaging markers of similarity to schizophrenia patterns. We modeled the relationship between medication dosage, age of onset, chronicity, symptom severity, and cognitive performance and FNC.

ResultsOur analysis identified a reliable schizophrenia-FNC signature characterized by aberrantly stronger negative cerebellothalamic and positive thalamocortical connectivity, implicating sensory, motor, and associative cortical circuits. While medication and chronicity were significantly associated with these signatures, the core cerebellothalamic disruptions remained a robust marker of schizophrenia.

ConclusionsThis work represents the largest schizophrenia-specific rsfMRI study to date, refines existing theoretical frameworks with a more nuanced map of how clinical variables interact with brain connectivity, and provides a high-fidelity template of schizophrenia-related connectivity. We have released this template as an open-access resource to facilitate reproducibility and accelerate the development of reliable rsfMRI-based schizophrenia biomarkers.
