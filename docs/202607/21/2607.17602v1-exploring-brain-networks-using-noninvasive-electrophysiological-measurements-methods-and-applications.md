## 摘要
脑电图（EEG）和脑磁图（MEG）以毫秒级时间分辨率提供大脑活动的无创测量，从而能够研究大规模脑网络内的功能性和有效性相互作用。本章全面概述了基于EEG/MEG的脑网络分析的方法论基础和实用工作流程。我们首先回顾了EEG和MEG的物理原理，强调了它们互补的优势和局限性。然后，我们描述了正问题和逆问题，包括个体头模型、源重建技术以及准确解剖建模对于可靠源定位的重要性。讨论了减轻容积传导和信号泄漏的策略，以及源空间连接性分析的最佳实践。本章回顾了广泛使用的功能连接和有效连接度量，包括相干性、相位同步度量、包络幅度相关性、格兰杰因果性、动态因果建模和传递熵，强调了它们的假设、优势和局限性。介绍了现代端到端分析流程，特别强调了Brainstorm和互补的开源软件，用于可重复的EEG/MEG研究。最后，我们讨论了新兴方法，包括时变连接性、跨频交互和基于网络的分析，阐明了无创电生理学如何有助于理解健康和疾病中的大脑组织。本章为寻求使用EEG和MEG绘制和解释人脑网络的研究人员和高级学生提供了概念基础和实践指导。

## Abstract
Electroencephalography (EEG) and magnetoencephalography (MEG) provide noninvasive measurements of brain activity with millisecond temporal resolution, enabling the investigation of functional and effective interactions within large-scale brain networks. This chapter presents a comprehensive overview of the methodological foundations and practical workflows for EEG/MEG-based brain network analysis. We first review the physical principles underlying EEG and MEG, emphasizing their complementary strengths and limitations. We then describe the forward and inverse problems, including subject-specific head modeling, source reconstruction techniques, and the importance of accurate anatomical modeling for reliable source localization. Strategies for mitigating volume conduction and signal leakage are discussed, together with best practices for source-space connectivity analysis. The chapter reviews widely used functional and effective connectivity measures, including coherence, phase synchronization metrics, amplitude envelope correlation, Granger causality, dynamic causal modeling, and transfer entropy, highlighting their assumptions, advantages, and limitations. Modern end-to-end analysis pipelines are presented, with particular emphasis on Brainstorm and complementary open-source software for reproducible EEG/MEG research. Finally, we discuss emerging approaches, including time-varying connectivity, cross-frequency interactions, and network-based analyses, illustrating how noninvasive electrophysiology contributes to understanding brain organization in health and disease. The chapter provides both conceptual foundations and practical guidance for researchers and advanced students seeking to map and interpret human brain networks using EEG and MEG.
