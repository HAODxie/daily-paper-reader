## 摘要
睡眠障碍与神经退行性疾病形成双向恶性循环。在慢波睡眠期间，类淋巴过程被认为有助于清除代谢废物，包括与阿尔茨海默病（AD）相关的蛋白质。衰老，尤其是神经退行性变，与慢波活动（SWA）的减少有关，这可能损害这些过程。在SWA中，慢振荡（SO, <1 Hz）及其与睡眠纺锤波的耦合捕捉了睡眠微结构的关键方面。睡眠期间的非侵入性脑刺激已成为调节这些动态的潜在方法；然而，关于这种调节与AD生物标志物关联的人类证据仍然稀少。

在这项探索性机制研究中，十名健康老年人接受了一晚慢振荡经颅电流刺激（so-tDCS）和一夜假刺激，采用随机交叉设计，随后连续五晚刺激。刺激在夜间前半段的NREM睡眠（N2/N3）期间进行。评估了隔夜和纵向的血浆磷酸化tau（p-tau）181、β-淀粉样蛋白（Aβ）42、Aβ40和总tau。脑电图分析量化了SO功率和SO-纺锤波耦合。由于研究的探索性，分析强调了效应量估计和解释方差。

So-tDCS诱导了SO功率和SO-纺锤波耦合的小到中度增加。与假刺激相比，刺激后隔夜血浆p-tau181增加。SO功率的增加与p-tau181变化呈强正相关，解释了大部分个体间差异。相比之下，SO-纺锤波耦合相位向SO上升期的偏移与隔夜Aβ42和Aβ40的增加以及Aβ42和Aβ42/40比率的纵向下降相关。

睡眠期间增强慢振荡动力学与外周AD生物标志物的变化相关。SO功率和SO-纺锤波耦合时间的差异性关联表明与tau和Aβ动力学的部分不同联系。这些发现支持睡眠微结构作为潜在的干预靶点，并应在更大规模队列中得到确认。

## Abstract
Sleep disturbances and neurodegeneration form a bidirectional vicious cycle. During slow-wave sleep, glymphatic processes are thought to facilitate the clearance of metabolic waste, including proteins implicated in Alzheimers disease (AD). Aging, and more prominently neurodegeneration, is associated with reductions in slow-wave activity (SWA), which may impair these processes. Within SWA, slow oscillations (SO, <1 Hz) and their coupling to sleep spindles capture key aspects of sleep microstructure. Non-invasive brain stimulation during sleep has emerged as a potential approach to modulate these dynamics; however, human evidence linking such modulation to AD biomarkers remains scarce.

In this exploratory mechanistic study, ten healthy older adults underwent one night of slow-oscillatory transcranial current stimulation (so-tDCS) and one sham night in a randomized crossover design, followed by five consecutive stimulation nights. Stimulation was applied during NREM sleep (N2/N3) in the first half of the night. Plasma phosphorylated tau (p-tau)181, {beta}-amyloid (A{beta})42, A{beta}40, and total tau were assessed overnight and longitudinally. EEG analyses quantified SO power and SO-spindle coupling. Due to the exploratory character of the study, analyses emphasized effect size estimation and explained variance.

So-tDCS induced small-to-moderate increases in SO power and SO-spindle coupling. Overnight increases in plasma p-tau181 were observed following stimulation relative to sham. Increases in SO power were strongly and positively associated with p-tau181 changes, explaining a substantial proportion of inter-individual variance. In contrast, shifts in SO-spindle coupling phase toward the SO up-state were associated with overnight increases in A{beta}42 and A{beta}40 and with longitudinal decreases in A{beta}42 and the A{beta}42/40 ratio.

Enhancing slow oscillatory dynamics during sleep is associated with changes in peripheral AD biomarkers. Differential associations for SO power and SO-spindle coupling timing suggest partially distinct links to tau and A{beta} dynamics. These findings support sleep microstructure as a potential intervention target, and should be confirmed in larger cohorts.
