## 摘要
视听语义匹配可以通过文字或图片实现，但这些格式是否涉及具有相似时间动态的共享语义匹配表征仍不清楚。我们记录了27名参与者执行视听语义匹配任务时的脑电图，任务中口头词汇与文字或图片配对。刺激包括自然物体和人造物体。采用时间分辨多变量模式分析（MVPA或解码）、交叉解码和时间泛化分析来表征语义处理的时间动态。在文字和图片条件下均出现了匹配与不匹配判断的可靠解码。文字条件下解码开始显著高于机会水平的时间早于图片条件，交叉解码分析揭示了文字和图片格式之间的成功泛化。时间泛化分析进一步展示了不同格式间不同的表征动态：文字处理以主要时间特异性的神经表征为特征，而图片处理则表现出更持续和时间稳定的表征。此外，两种格式下自然物体的匹配相关辨别出现时间均早于人造物体。结果表明，语音-文字匹配比语音-图片匹配表现出更早的视听对齐神经证据，这可能反映了听觉语言输入与视觉信息在不同表征格式下整合方式的差异。

## Abstract
Audiovisual semantic matching can be achieved using either written words or pictures, yet whether these formats engage shared semantic matching representations with similar temporal dynamics remains unclear. We recorded electroencephalography from 27 participants while they performed audiovisual semantic matching tasks in which spoken words were paired with either written words or pictures. Stimuli included both natural and man-made objects. Time-resolved multivariate pattern analyses (MVPA or decoding), cross-decoding, and temporal generalization analyses were used to characterize the temporal dynamics of semantic processing. Reliable decoding of matching versus mismatching judgments emerged in both word and picture conditions. Decoding onset that significant above chance level occurred earlier for written words than for pictures and cross-decoding analyses revealed successful generalization between word and picture formats. Temporal generalization analyses further demonstrated distinct representational dynamics across formats, with word processing characterized by predominantly time-specific neural representations and picture processing showing more sustained and temporally stable representations. In addition, matching-related discrimination emerged earlier for natural objects than for man-made objects across both formats. The results suggest that speech-word matching shows earlier neural evidence of audiovisual alignment than speech-picture matching, potentially reflecting differences in how auditory linguistic input is integrated with visual information across representational formats.
