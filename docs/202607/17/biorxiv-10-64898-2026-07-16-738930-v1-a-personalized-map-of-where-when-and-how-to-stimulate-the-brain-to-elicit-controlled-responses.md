## 摘要
脑刺激已彻底改变了几种神经和精神疾病的治疗，并广泛用于揭示人脑中的因果相互作用。然而，即使刺激参数保持一致，其效果也会因试验而异。识别这种变异性的来源并制定减少变异性的新策略是实现更可控干预的关键。然而，在真实实验中进行全面测试是不可行的，因为每个参与者只能在实验者无法完全控制的条件下对少数几个部位进行探测。在这里，我们使用个性化脑模型来绘制刺激反应如何随目标部位和大脑当前状态而变化。我们表明，刺激反应由目标沿皮层单模态到跨模态层级的位置以及大脑的全局当前状态共同决定，较低的活动状态产生较大的反应。因此，将刺激时机对准低活动期可减少反应幅度在试验间的变异性。即使没有状态信息，与单独刺激任一区域相比，联合刺激特定区域对可减少变异性。这些发现共同确定了在何处、何时以及如何刺激大脑以获得更可重复的反应，为闭环和回路级刺激方案提供了具体预测。

## Abstract
Brain stimulation has transformed the treatment of several neurological and psychiatric disorders and is now widely used to read out causal interactions in the human brain. However, its effects vary from one trial to the next, even when the stimulation parameters are kept identical. Identifying the sources of this variability and developing new strategies to reduce it are key to achieving more controllable interventions. Yet, exhaustive testing in real experiments is unfeasible, because each participant can only be probed at a handful of sites under conditions the experimenter cannot fully control. Here we use personalized brain models to map how stimulation responses vary across target sites and with the brain's ongoing state. We show that stimulation responses are jointly determined by the target's position along the cortical unimodal-to-transmodal hierarchy and the brain's global ongoing activity, with lower-activity states yielding larger responses. Accordingly, timing stimulation to low-activity periods reduces trial-to-trial variability in response magnitude. Even without state information, joint stimulation of specific region pairs reduces variability compared with stimulating either region alone. Together, these findings identify where, when, and how to stimulate the brain to achieve more reproducible responses, with concrete predictions for closed-loop and circuit-level stimulation protocols.
