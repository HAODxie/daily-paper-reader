## 摘要
海马体接收来自多个感觉系统的汇聚输入，但在人类中，它几乎完全通过视觉进行研究。这里我们研究海马体如何促进超越视觉模态的感觉处理，以及视觉信息与这些其他模态的多感觉整合。参与者多次观看简短的自然电影片段，每个片段以四种形式呈现：仅听觉、仅视觉、一致视听（音视频来自同一电影）和不一致视听（音视频来自不同电影）。使用高分辨率fMRI，我们测量了沿人类海马体长轴和亚区的单变量激活和多变量表征。单变量分析仅在海马体各亚区检测到视觉反应，没有听觉刺激的激活，也没有一致刺激的增益；而多变量分析揭示了听觉和视觉场景的稳健表征。与单感觉刺激相比，后海马体对一致刺激表现出增强的模式相似性，尤其在左半球，表明多感觉促进。前海马体显示出同一片段听觉和视觉版本之间的跨模态解码，由右半球驱动，提示更抽象的表征。最后，全脑搜索光分析在已知支持多感觉整合的皮层区域发现了平行效应。这些发现推进了对人类海马体中听觉和多感觉编码的理解，揭示了其沿长轴的特异化和偏侧化。

## Abstract
The hippocampus receives convergent input from multiple sensory systems, yet in humans it has been studied almost exclusively through vision. Here we examine how the hippocampus contributes to sensory processing beyond the visual modality and to the multisensory integration of visual information with these other modalities. Participants were exposed to multiple repetitions of short naturalistic movie clips, each presented in four formats: auditory-only, visual-only, congruent audiovisual, and incongruent audiovisual (audio and video from different movies). Using high-resolution fMRI, we measured univariate activation and multivariate representations across the longitudinal axis and subfields of the human hippocampus. Whereas univariate analyses detected only visual responses across hippocampal subregions, with no activation for auditory stimuli and no benefit of congruent stimuli, multivariate analyses revealed robust representations of both auditory and visual scenes. The posterior hippocampus showed enhanced pattern similarity for congruent stimuli relative to unisensory stimuli, especially in the left hemisphere, demonstrating multisensory facilitation. The anterior hippocampus showed crossmodal decoding between auditory and visual versions of the same clip, driven by the right hemisphere, suggesting a more abstract representation. Finally, whole-brain searchlight analyses revealed parallel effects in cortical regions known to support multisensory integration. These findings advance understanding of auditory and multisensory coding in the human hippocampus, revealing specialization and lateralization along its longitudinal axis.
