## 摘要
基于先前对等频与跨频耦合的观察，我们评估了如下假设：等频和跨频相位同步可能表征大脑感觉运动处理的整合-分离视角。利用在正常条件以及感觉输入减少条件（闭眼清醒、睡眠和昏迷，此时神经网络中感觉运动处理的功能分离程度可能较低）下获取的脑部记录，我们评估了与跨频同步相关的同步状态空间划分的潜在差异。在感觉输入减少的条件下发现了更多的划分。此外，与等频耦合相比，跨频耦合的同步状态空间复杂度较低，即连接配置较少。这些结果支持以下观点：等频耦合有利于多个脑区在复杂的同步状态空间中进行整合，该空间充满了可能的连接配置；而跨频耦合则有助于分离，即发生在特定脑区的局部感觉运动转换。这一证据可能有助于重新审视多频率关系在神经活动中备受讨论的作用，以及神经系统的结构和功能模块化组织如何产生意识所需协调活动以及复杂环境中适当的认知行为。

## Abstract
Derived from previous observations on equal and cross-frequency coupling, we evaluated the proposal that equal and cross-frequency phase synchronization may characterize the integration-segregation perspective of cerebral sensory-motor processing. Using brain recordings obtained in normal conditions and in conditions of diminished sensory input (eyes closed wakefulness, sleep and coma, when there is presumably less functional segregation of sensory-motor processing in neural networks), we assessed potential differences in partitioning of the synchrony state space linked to cross-frequency synchronization. More partitions were found in conditions of decreased sensory input. In addition, there was a less complex synchrony state space in cross-frequency as compared with equal-frequency coupling, in terms of fewer connectivity configurations. These results support the idea that equal-frequency coupling favours integration from multiple brain regions occurring in a complex synchrony state space rich in possible connectivity configurations, whereas cross-frequency coupling contributes to segregation, or localized sensory-motor transformations taking place in specific brain areas. This evidence may contribute to new considerations about the much-discussed role of multi-frequency relations in neuronal activity, and how the structural and functional modular organization of the nervous system is able to generate the coordinated activity needed for consciousness and appropriate cognitive behaviors in complex environments.
