## 摘要
灰质和脑室空间中fMRI信号的时间协调代表了大脑生理学中一个新兴但尚未被充分理解的维度，可能与神经保护相关。在这里，我们分析了来自880名参与者的2177次静息态fMRI扫描，这些参与者覆盖了从正常衰老到痴呆的认知谱系，采用了一种统一的相位耦合方法，该结合了脑组织和脑室空间的波动。我们识别出随时间发生的不同脑室（BraVe）耦合模式，并比较了它们在不同认知状态组、神经退行性生物标志物和认知评分中的时间占有度。

在最常见的BraVe模式中，整个大脑的信号与脑室信号反相位演变，随着认知衰退而出现频率降低（p < 10-7，g > 0.3）。在其余的BraVe模式中，特定的皮层区域暂时与脑室波动对齐，在皮层水平上形成经典的静息态网络。两种与默认模式网络和额顶网络重叠的BraVe模式在痴呆中出现的频率较低（p < 10-8，g > 0.35），后者与FDG-PET葡萄糖代谢显著相关（r = 0.20，p = 3.65x10-5）。相反，一种与边缘网络重叠的模式其占有度随认知衰退而增加，并与APOE ε4状态相关（r = 0.11，p = 1.30x10-6）。

BraVe模式占有度与认知功能的相关性比与疾病特异性分子生物标志物的相关性更强且更广泛，每个模式有16至27个认知和功能变量经受住了严格的Bonferroni校正，涵盖全局认知、情景记忆、执行功能和日常功能。这些发现揭示了静息态网络动力学背后的瞬时相位偏移并非随意：当皮层子网络从全局大脑信号中移出相位时，它们与脑室信号对齐，这表明静息态功能连接和脑室耦合可能是同一基本物理过程的两个视角，该过程与认知功能直接相关。

## Abstract
The temporal coordination between fMRI signals in gray matter and ventricular spaces represents an emerging yet poorly understood dimension of brain physiology with potential relevance for neuroprotection. Here, we analyzed 2177 resting-state fMRI scans from 880 participants spanning the cognitive spectrum from normal aging to dementia, using a unified phase-coupling approach that incorporates fluctuations in both brain tissue and ventricular space. We identify distinct modes of Brain-Ventricle (BraVe) coupling occurring over time, and compare their temporal occupancy across cognitive status groups, neurodegenerative biomarkers and cognitive scores.

In the most prevalent BraVe mode, signals across the entire brain evolve in anti-phase with ventricular signals, occurring less frequently with cognitive decline (p < 10-7, g > 0.3). In the remaining BraVe modes, specific cortical regions temporarily align with ventricular fluctuations, shaping canonical resting-state networks at the cortical level. Two BraVe modes overlapping with the Default Mode Network and the Frontoparietal Network, occur less frequently in dementia (p < 10-8, g > 0.35), with the latter correlating significantly with FDG-PET glucose metabolism (r = 0.20, p = 3.65x10-5). Conversely, a mode overlapping with the Limbic Network increases in occupancy with cognitive decline and associates with APOE {varepsilon}4 status (r = 0.11, p = 1.30x10-6).

BraVe mode occupancy correlates more strongly and broadly with cognitive function than with disease-specific molecular biomarkers, with between 16 and 27 cognitive and functional variables per mode surviving strict Bonferroni correction, spanning global cognition, episodic memory, executive function, and everyday functioning. These findings reveal that the transient phase shifts underlying resting-state network dynamics are not arbitrary: when cortical subnetworks shift out of phase from the global brain signal, they align with ventricular signals, suggesting that resting-state functional connectivity and brain-ventricle coupling may be two perspectives on the same underlying physical process, one that is directly linked with cognitive function.
