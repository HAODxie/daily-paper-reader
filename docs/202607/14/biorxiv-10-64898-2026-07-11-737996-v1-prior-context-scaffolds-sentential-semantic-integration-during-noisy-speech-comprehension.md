## 摘要
理解嘈杂语音是日常交流的核心挑战，但听众通常借助先验语境成功应对。大脑如何利用这种语境仍存在争议：它可能细化对即将出现单词的预测，也可能提供一个更高层次的框架，帮助退化的语音连贯成意义。在此，我们结合同步EEG-fNIRS记录与分层多变量编码模型，追踪先验语境如何从声学到单词再到句子意义塑造语音处理。参与者在清晰语音、嘈杂语音和语境支持的嘈杂语音条件下聆听自然叙述语音。语境使嘈杂语音的理解接近清晰语音水平。EEG显示，语境支持降低了词汇惊奇度和熵的神经编码，表明对局部单词级预测需求的追踪减弱。相反，在有语境时，fNIRS显示额叶区域和右侧角回对句子级语义整合的编码增强，且更强的角回编码能预测更好的理解。通过结合EEG和fNIRS捕捉互补的电生理和血液动力学信号，这种多模态方法揭示了退化语音理解中的层次性转变：先验语境不仅仅改善逐词预测，而是支撑嘈杂输入整合为连贯的话语。

## Abstract
Understanding speech in noise is a central challenge of everyday communication, yet listeners often succeed by using prior context. How the brain uses such context remains debated: it may refine predictions about upcoming words, or it may provide a higher-level framework that helps degraded speech cohere into meaning. Here we combined simultaneous EEG-fNIRS recording with hierarchical multivariate encoding models to track how prior context shapes speech processing from acoustics to words and sentential meaning. Participants listened to natural spoken narratives under clear speech, noisy speech, and context-supported noisy speech conditions. Context brought comprehension of noisy speech close to clear-speech levels. EEG revealed that contextual support reduced neural encoding of lexical surprisal and entropy, indicating weaker tracking of local word-level prediction demands. In contrast, when context was available, fNIRS showed enhanced encoding of sentence-level semantic integration across frontal regions and the right angular gyrus, and stronger angular gyrus encoding predicted better comprehension. By combining EEG and fNIRS to capture complementary electrophysiological and hemodynamic signals, this multimodal approach reveals a hierarchical shift in degraded speech comprehension: prior context does not simply improve word-by-word prediction, but scaffolds the integration of noisy input into coherent discourse.
