## 摘要
新生儿静息态功能连接可能为后来Q-CHAT评分的变异提供早期标记，这些评分在非临床人群中按维度测量，但携带最稳健预测信号的大规模系统仍不清楚。利用来自发展人类连接组计划（277名足月儿、120名早产儿）的397名婴儿的静息态fMRI数据，我们应用了稳定性驱动的、ROI约束的连接组预测建模框架来预测18个月幼儿自闭症定量检查表（Q-CHAT）评分。在整个人群和足月儿中观察到显著预测，但在仅早产儿组中未达到统计学显著性。在统计显著的模型（整个人群和足月儿）中，最突出的枢纽位于枕叶及邻近皮质区域，包括枕中回、舌回、距状裂和罗兰迪克岛盖。在网络层面，最强的预测连接将视觉和视觉-联想系统与听觉网络联系起来，并受到内侧运动、颞顶和前额叶系统的额外贡献。这些发现表明，后期Q-CHAT评分的变异与新生儿大规模功能组织有关，特别是在感觉和多感觉通路中。

## Abstract
Neonatal resting-state functional connectivity may provide early markers of later variation in Q-CHAT scores, measured dimensionally within a non-clinical population, but the large-scale systems carrying the most robust predictive signal remain unclear. Using resting-state fMRI data from 397 infants in the Developing Human Connectome Project (277 term-born, 120 preterm-born), we applied a stability-driven, ROI-constrained connectome-based predictive modeling framework to predict 18-month Quantitative Checklist for Autism in Toddlers (Q-CHAT) scores. Significant prediction was observed in the whole cohort and in term-born infants, but did not reach statistical significance in the preterm-only group. Across the statistically significant models (whole cohort and term-born infants), the most prominent hubs were located in occipital and adjacent cortical regions, including the middle occipital gyrus, lingual gyrus, calcarine gyrus, and rolandic operculum. At the network level, the strongest predictive connections linked visual and visual-association systems with auditory networks, with additional contributions from medial motor, temporoparietal, and prefrontal systems. These findings suggest that later variation in Q-CHAT scores, is associated with neonatal large-scale functional organization, particularly in sensory and multisensory pathways.
